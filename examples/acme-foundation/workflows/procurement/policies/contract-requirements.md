# Contract Requirements

## Purpose

This policy specifies the mandatory provisions, insurance requirements, and
termination clauses that must be included in all vendor contracts executed by
the Acme Foundation. These requirements protect the Foundation's interests
and ensure compliance with nonprofit governance standards.

## Required Contract Clauses

All vendor contracts must include the following provisions:

1. **Scope of Work:** A clear, detailed description of the goods or services
   to be delivered, including quantities, specifications, and acceptance
   criteria.

2. **Payment Terms:** Net-30 payment terms are standard. Advance payments
   exceeding 25% of the contract value require Finance Director approval.
   Milestone-based payment schedules are preferred for contracts over $10,000.

3. **Term and Renewal:** Explicit start and end dates. Automatic renewal
   clauses are prohibited unless approved by the Finance Director. Multi-year
   contracts must include annual performance review provisions.

4. **Indemnification:** The vendor must indemnify and hold harmless the
   Foundation, its officers, directors, and employees from claims arising
   from the vendor's performance or negligence.

5. **Confidentiality:** Vendors handling Foundation data, donor information,
   or program participant records must agree to confidentiality provisions
   consistent with the Foundation's data protection policy.

6. **Non-Discrimination:** The vendor must comply with all applicable
   federal, state, and local non-discrimination laws and the Foundation's
   own non-discrimination policy.

7. **Audit Rights:** The Foundation reserves the right to audit vendor records
   related to contract performance for a period of three years following
   contract completion.

8. **Governing Law:** All contracts are governed by the laws of the state in
   which the Foundation is incorporated.

## Insurance Requirements

Vendors must maintain the following minimum insurance coverage:

- **Commercial General Liability:** $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000
  aggregate. The Acme Foundation must be named as an additional insured.
- **Workers' Compensation:** Statutory limits as required by applicable state
  law, with employer's liability of at least $500,000.
- **Professional Liability (if applicable):** $1,000,000 per occurrence for
  vendors providing professional or consulting services.
- **Cyber Liability (if applicable):** $1,000,000 for vendors with access to
  Foundation IT systems or data.

Certificates of insurance must be provided before contract execution and
renewed annually for the duration of the contract.

## Termination Provisions

All contracts must include the following termination rights:

- **Termination for Convenience:** The Foundation may terminate any contract
  with 30 days' written notice. The vendor is entitled to payment for work
  satisfactorily completed through the termination date.
- **Termination for Cause:** Either party may terminate immediately upon
  written notice if the other party materially breaches the contract and fails
  to cure within 15 days of written notice.
- **Termination for Insolvency:** The Foundation may terminate immediately if
  the vendor files for bankruptcy, becomes insolvent, or ceases operations.
- **Transition Assistance:** For contracts involving ongoing services, the
  vendor must provide reasonable transition assistance for up to 60 days
  following termination.

## Signature Authority

Contracts must be signed by an authorized representative of the Foundation:

- **Up to $5,000:** Procurement Lead
- **$5,001 - $25,000:** Finance Director
- **Over $25,000:** Executive Director or Board Chair, following Board approval

## Record Retention

Executed contracts and all supporting procurement documentation must be
retained for a minimum of seven years following contract completion, consistent
with the Foundation's record retention schedule and IRS requirements for
501(c)(3) organizations.
