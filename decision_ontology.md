The interesting question this raises is: what's the ontology of a judgment point? What are its components? Off the top of my head, I'd say something like:

- The decision type (approval, classification, prioritization, allocation, exception handling...)
- The inputs (documents, data, prior decisions, contextual information)
- The governing policies (scoped by domain and decision type)
- The authority model (who can make this decision, under what conditions can it be delegated)
- The precedent (how has this type of decision been made before)
- The output schema (what does the decision produce — a yes/no, a ranking, a modified document, a routing choice)
- The accountability trail (who decided, when, based on what, and what was the reasoning)
