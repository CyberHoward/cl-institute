# Vendor Preference Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes the Acme Foundation's preferences for vendor selection
in procurement decisions. It balances cost efficiency with the Foundation's
commitment to local economic development and long-term vendor relationships.

## Existing Vendor Preference

When evaluating proposals for goods or services, preference should be given to
vendors with whom the Foundation has an existing relationship, provided that:

- The existing vendor's pricing is within **15%** of the lowest qualifying bid.
- The existing vendor has maintained a satisfactory performance record, defined
  as no unresolved complaints and on-time delivery for at least 90% of prior
  orders in the trailing 12 months.
- The existing vendor remains in good standing with all compliance requirements
  (current insurance, no sanctions flags, valid W-9 on file).

If the cost delta exceeds 15%, the Procurement Lead must document the rationale
for either selecting the incumbent or switching vendors.

## Local Sourcing Preference

The Foundation encourages procurement from vendors headquartered or operating
within the Foundation's primary service area (a 50-mile radius of the
Foundation's offices). Local vendors receive a preference when:

- Their bid is within **10%** of the lowest non-local bid.
- They meet all technical and compliance requirements.
- The procurement is for goods or services where local availability does not
  compromise quality or delivery timelines.

## Minority and Women-Owned Business Preference

Consistent with the Foundation's commitment to equity, vendors certified as
minority-owned (MBE), women-owned (WBE), or disadvantaged business enterprises
(DBE) receive a preference when their bid is within **10%** of the lowest
qualifying bid.

## Documentation Requirements

All vendor preference decisions must be documented in the vendor assessment
report, including:

- The specific preference applied and the basis for application.
- The cost differential and how it compares to the applicable threshold.
- Any factors that override the preference (e.g., delivery timeline, technical
  capability, compliance concerns).

## Exceptions

This policy does not apply to emergency procurement, sole-source contracts
with documented justification, or purchases under $500.
